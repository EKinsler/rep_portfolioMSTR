FLAGSTAFF
         

REPUTATIONS AND JOB OPPORTUNITIES


Flagstaff has the 386th largest population in the United States. The population grew by 6.4% from 2010 to 2015, which was faster than the national average. Unemployment in the Flagstaff area is 5.7%, which is higher than the national average, and is getting better. Flagstaff has the 288th largest local economy in the US, and a high cost of living. The average pay for jobs in Flagstaff is below average at $41,550, and the median cost of a home in Flagstaff is $263,000, which is above the national average. 42% of workers in Flagstaff hold a bachelors degree or higher (above average), and 91% of workers have a high school degree (above average). Driving to work is especially common in Flagstaff. 68% of Flagstaff workers drive to work alone, 1% take public transit, 12% carpool, and 17% walk. People in Flagstaff spend an average of 15 minutes commuting to work, which is shorter than the national average of 26 minutes.
